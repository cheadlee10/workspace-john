1. Why the Current Email Fails (Diagnosis)
The outreach email sent to Done Right Works has six critical failures that guarantee it will be deleted, ignored, or flagged as spam. Each one is fixable. None are optional.
Failure #1: Broken Character Encoding
The email displays "I?ll" instead of "I'll" and "won?t" instead of "won't". This happens when curly/smart quotes from Google Docs, ChatGPT, or Word get pasted into a plain-text email field that does not support UTF-8. This is the single biggest red flag that a bot or automation tool sent this email. A business owner seeing garbled characters will immediately assume this is spam and delete it.
Failure #2: Zero Visual Proof
You are selling a website -- a visual product -- using nothing but a wall of plain text. This is like selling a house with no photos. The highest-performing web design cold emails include either a screenshot mockup, a preview of a proposed redesign, or a link to a live demo. This email has none of these.
Failure #3: Feature Dump Instead of Value
The email has a "What we build" section listing features: "Enterprise-level website," "Mobile-first quote flow," "Local SEO setup." Nobody reading a cold email from a stranger cares about your feature list. They care about their own problems. The email should identify one specific problem with their current online presence and explain the business impact. Features come later in a follow-up conversation -- not in the first touch.
Failure #4: Pricing in a First-Touch Email
The subject line mentions $299 / $25 / $100 SEO, but the email body shows blank amounts. Beyond the technical bug, putting pricing in the very first cold email gives the prospect a reason to say no before they have seen any value. The goal of the first email is to start a conversation, not close a deal. Pricing belongs in email #2 or #3, after engagement.
Failure #5: No Social Proof
There is not a single piece of evidence that NorthStar Synergy has ever built a website for anyone. No client names, no results, no portfolio link, no testimonials. Business owners want to see relatable examples with real results before they engage.
Failure #6: Generic AI-Sounding Copy
"I reviewed your online presence and spotted a straightforward growth opportunity" -- this sentence is generated by every AI cold email tool on the market. Business owners receive dozens of emails with this exact structure. It signals zero genuine research. The opening line must reference something specific and verifiable about their actual business.

3. The Golden Rules (Non-Negotiable Standards)
These rules apply to every single outreach email. No exceptions. If any one is violated, the email does not get sent.
RULE 1: Plain Text Format for Cold Emails
Research confirms: for cold outreach to people who don't know you, plain text emails dramatically outperform HTML. HTML cold emails have 25% lower open and click-through rates, and spam filters flag them more aggressively. Use clean plain text with one link (screenshot URL). Save HTML for follow-up nurture after engagement.
RULE 2: Every Email Contains a Visual
Every outreach email must include either: (a) a direct URL link to a live preview site you built, OR (b) a link to a hosted screenshot mockup of a site for a similar business. Never send a web design pitch that is 100% text.
RULE 3: The Email is About THEM, Not About You
One short sentence about NorthStar Synergy is the maximum. The rest must be about the prospect's business, their specific situation, and what is in it for them. No feature lists. No 'What we build' sections.
RULE 4: One Specific Observation Proves You Looked
The opening must reference something specific and verifiable about the prospect's business. Examples: 'I noticed Done Right Works does not show up on the first page of Google for handyman Tacoma' or 'Your Yelp page has 47 reviews but your website does not link to any of them.'
RULE 5: No Pricing in Email #1
The first email exists to start a conversation, not close a deal. Pricing creates an immediate reason to say no. Pricing is introduced in Email #2 or #3, after the prospect has engaged and seen value.
RULE 6: Soft CTA Only
The call to action must be low-friction. Good: 'Want me to send a 2-minute walkthrough?' or 'Worth a quick look?' Bad: 'Reply preview and I will send it' or 'Schedule a call' (too much commitment for a stranger).
RULE 7: Under 100 Words
Short emails (under 100 words) have 50% higher open rate than longer ones. The prospect should read the entire email in 15 seconds on their phone without scrolling.
RULE 8: Zero Encoding Errors
Every email must be proofread for character encoding issues. All apostrophes must render correctly, not as question marks. Paste all copy through a plain-text editor (Notepad / TextEdit) BEFORE putting it into the email tool.
RULE 9: Professional Sender Identity
Send from a real name at a real domain (john@northstarsynergy.com), not gmail. Include a signature: Name, Title, Company, Phone.
RULE 10: Every Email Has a Follow-Up Sequence
No email goes out standalone. Every first touch has a pre-written sequence of 2-3 follow-ups on the 3-7-7 cadence (Day 0, Day 3, Day 10). Stopping after one email leaves 62.5% of possible replies on the table.

7. The Visual: Screenshot Mockup Process
Every email must include a visual. Here is the exact process.
Option A: Screenshot of Existing Portfolio Site (Fastest)
Step 1: Take a full-page screenshot (Chrome: Ctrl+Shift+P > "Capture full size screenshot").
Step 2: Go to screely.com (free) or shots.so (free tier).
Step 3: Upload screenshot. Select Chrome-style browser frame. Clean background.
Step 4: Export as PNG at 1200px wide minimum.
Step 5: Host on northstarsynergy.com/previews/ or free image host (Imgur, Cloudinary).
Step 6: Include the direct URL in the email as clickable text.
Option B: Spec/Demo Site for the Prospect (Gold Standard)
Build a quick landing page specifically for the prospect. Takes 30-60 minutes but has dramatically higher conversion because the prospect sees their own business name, services, and city on a professional site. If you have a template for this business category, clone and swap in minutes.
Option C: Link to Live Portfolio Piece (Minimum Acceptable)
At minimum, link to a live website you built for a similar business. Less personalized but infinitely better than zero visuals.
WHY THIS MATTERS: The "This Could Be Your New Website" approach is one of the highest-performing cold email strategies in web design. Showing -- not telling -- what you can do creates curiosity and makes the prospect feel like you have already invested effort in them.

9. Technical Checklist: Encoding, Deliverability, Compliance
Character Encoding Fix
The "?" problem is from curly quotes pasted from Google Docs/ChatGPT/Word. The fix:
1. Write your email in any tool.
2. BEFORE pasting into email tool, paste into Notepad (Win) or TextEdit Plain Text (Mac).
3. This strips all hidden formatting, smart quotes, em-dashes, and Unicode.
4. Copy from plain-text editor into your email tool.
5. Proofread visually. Search for "?" characters. If found, repeat.
6. Alternative: In Google Docs > Tools > Preferences > uncheck "Use smart quotes".
Deliverability Best Practices
- Warm up new domains: 5-10 emails/day for 2 weeks, then scale to 30-50/day max.
- Use dedicated sending domain (mail.northstarsynergy.com) to protect main domain.
- Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records. Without these, emails go to spam.
- Keep bounce rate below 5%. Verify addresses with Hunter.io, ZeroBounce, or NeverBounce.
- Do NOT use link shorteners (bit.ly) -- spam filters flag them.
- Avoid spam trigger words: "free," "limited time," "act now," "urgent," "guarantee."
- Include physical address and unsubscribe option (CAN-SPAM requirement).
- Monitor sender reputation via Google Postmaster Tools.
CAN-SPAM Compliance (Required by Law)
Violations: up to $50,120 per email.
- Do not use deceptive subject lines.
- Include valid physical business address.
- Include clear opt-out. "Reply no and I will stop" counts.
- Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days.
- If using a third party to send, you are still legally responsible.

11. Complete Workflow: Step-by-Step Process per Lead
This is the repeatable loop. Follow it for every prospect, every time.
STEP 1: RESEARCH THE PROSPECT (5-10 minutes)
- Google their business name + city. Note what page they appear on.
- Check if they have a website. If yes, test on mobile.
- Check Google Business Profile -- reviews, rating, website link.
- Check Yelp, Facebook, other listings.
- Write down ONE specific, verifiable observation.
- Identify owner/decision-maker first name.
STEP 2: PREPARE THE VISUAL (15-60 minutes)
- Option A (fastest): Screenshot existing portfolio site. Mockup via screely.com. Host it.
- Option B (medium): Clone template, swap business name/area/colors.
- Option C (gold): Build a quick landing page for this specific prospect.
- The link must be live and working BEFORE you write the email.
STEP 3: WRITE THE EMAIL (5 minutes)
- Choose template A, B, or C based on prospect situation.
- Fill in ALL variables with real data. No placeholders remaining.
- Paste through plain-text editor to strip encoding issues.
- Paste into email tool. Proofread for "?" characters.
STEP 4: WRITE FOLLOW-UPS (5-10 minutes)
- Follow-up #1 (Day 3): gentle bump + new insight.
- Follow-up #2 (Day 10): case study + re-share preview link.
- Breakup (Day 17): graceful exit with open door.
- Schedule all three in email tool.
STEP 5: PRE-SEND CHECKLIST (2 minutes)
- Go through every item in Section 10. If anything fails, fix before sending.
STEP 6: SEND
- Send Tuesday-Thursday, 9:30-11:00 AM or 1:30-3:00 PM local time.
- Log: Name, Company, Email, Date Sent, Template, Follow-up Status.
- Let automated follow-up sequence run.
- If they reply, STOP sequence immediately. Respond personally within 2 hours.
