# Objection Scripts — No-Site Local Business Sales

Use this structure: **Acknowledge → Reframe → Evidence → Close question**

## 1) "It’s too expensive."
"Totally fair. Most owners compare this to cost, but the better comparison is missed jobs from not being visible on Google. Even 1–2 extra jobs can cover the build cost. We can also start with a smaller scope and phase upgrades. Would you prefer Basic or Pro to start?"

## 2) "I only use Facebook."
"Facebook is great for people who already know you. New customers usually search Google first. A website lets you capture both channels instead of just one. Want me to show a simple setup that complements your Facebook page?"

## 3) "I don’t have time to manage a site."
"That’s exactly why clients hire us. We handle setup, updates, and fixes. You only approve content and we run the rest. If the workload is near-zero for you, does this become a fit?"

## 4) "Send info, I’ll review later."
"Happy to send it. To make it useful, let’s lock a 10-minute slot so I can answer questions live and avoid back-and-forth. Is {{TimeA}} or {{TimeB}} better?"

## 5) "My friend/nephew can do it."
"That can work if they can deliver quickly and maintain it long-term. Most owners come to us after delays. If speed and reliability matter, we can have you live in days, not months. Want to compare side-by-side options?"

## 6) "I need to think about it."
"Makes sense. Usually it comes down to one thing: budget, timing, or confidence in outcome. Which one should we solve first?"

## 7) "Call me later / bad time."
"No problem. What’s the best 10-minute window later today or tomorrow so I can be brief and useful?"

## 8) "We already have a website."
"Got it. If it’s okay, I can run a quick visibility check and show where leads may be leaking (mobile speed, conversion path, local search). If there’s no upside, we stop there. Worth a quick review?"

## 9) "Do you have examples?"
"Yes — I’ll send examples relevant to your industry and size. Quickest way to evaluate fit is a short walkthrough so you can ask direct questions. Can we book 10 minutes?"

## 10) "Can you do it cheaper?"
"I can reduce scope to hit budget, but I don’t cut quality-critical items. We can start with the core package now and add enhancements later. Which budget range are you trying to stay within?"

---

## Closing Prompts (always ask one)
- "Should we reserve your launch slot for this week?"
- "Would you like Basic, Pro, or Premium?"
- "Want to start with the smaller package and scale after launch?"
- "Is there anything preventing a yes today?"
