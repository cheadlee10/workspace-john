# Restaurant Website Market Opportunity Research
## Compiled March 2026

---

## 1. MARKET SIZE

### Total Restaurants in the US
- **$1.55 trillion** in total US restaurant/foodservice sales projected for 2026 (National Restaurant Association)
- **~750,000+** restaurant establishments in the US (varies by definition; 152,430 single-location full-service + 142,206 chain restaurants + fast food + fast casual + other categories)
- **7 in 10 restaurants are single-unit (independent) operations** -- this is the primary target market
- **9 in 10 restaurants have fewer than 50 employees**
- Independent and small chains make up **89% of full-service restaurants**

### Restaurants WITHOUT Websites
- **19% of restaurant operators do NOT have a website** (TouchBistro 2026 State of Restaurants Report -- surveyed 600+ operators)
- **Only 69% of full-service restaurants maintain a website** (separate 2025 source), while 99% have social media -- meaning **31% of full-service restaurants have NO website** but are active on social media
- Older data (TechCrunch): 95% of independent restaurants didn't have mobile sites, only 40% had online menus -- this has improved but independent restaurants still lag significantly
- For small businesses overall: **28% of US small businesses lack a website** (2023 survey), and **58% of those without sites plan to get one in 2026**

### Restaurants WITHOUT Online Menus
- As of 2021, only about **50-54% of full-service restaurants** had shifted menus online
- **88% of restaurants** said they would consider swapping to digital menus -- meaning many still haven't done it
- **79% of customers prefer online ordering and kiosks** -- massive gap between demand and supply

### Calculated Total Addressable Market (TAM)
Using conservative estimates:
- ~750,000 restaurants in the US
- 19-31% without websites = **142,500 to 232,500 restaurants without any website**
- If you charge $150/month average: **$256M - $418M annual TAM** just for website services
- Including food trucks (92,000+) and pop-ups: TAM expands further
- Including online ordering, marketing tools, and premium features: TAM easily exceeds **$500M-$1B annually**

## 2. REVENUE IMPACT

### With Website vs. Without
- **77% of diners** visit a restaurant's website before deciding to dine or order (MGH survey)
- **68% of diners have been discouraged** from visiting a restaurant because of a poor website
- **31% of US shoppers chose NOT to shop at a small business** because it didn't have a website
- Small businesses with websites **grow roughly 2x faster** than those without
- A **one-star increase in Yelp rating** leads to a **5-9% increase in revenue** -- websites help manage this reputation

### Online Ordering Revenue Impact
- Adding online ordering increases sales by an average of **18%** (TouchBistro 2026 State of Restaurants Report)
- Online orders can contribute to revenue growth of approximately **30%**
- Third-party delivery partnerships raise restaurant sales volume by **10-20%**
- **Average digital order value is 23% higher** than in-person transactions
- **25% of consumers spend MORE on off-premise orders** vs dine-in
- Online order tabs average **30% higher** than telephone or over-the-counter transactions
- **75% of QSR sales** now generated through online and phone orders
- Full-service restaurants have seen a **237% increase in digital orders** since 2020

### Direct vs. Third-Party Ordering
- Restaurants lose **25-30% per month** on third-party delivery providers (DoorDash, UberEats, Grubhub)
- DoorDash commissions: 15% (Basic), 25% (Plus), 30% (Premier)
- True cost of third-party delivery can exceed **40% of revenue** including all fees
- **67% of consumers prefer** to use a restaurant's OWN website or app for food delivery
- Restaurants save **35% per order** by using owned online ordering vs third-party
- Having a direct ordering channel is essentially a **25-30% margin recapture** opportunity

### Average Restaurant Revenue Context
- Small independent restaurants: **$250,000 - $1.5M** annual revenue
- Average across all types: **~$486,000** annually
- Full-service restaurants average: **$1.2M** annually
- Quick-service restaurants average: **$950,000** annually
- Profit margins typically: **3-10%**
- Average restaurant ROI: **~11%** (2024)

### Revenue Impact Summary
If a restaurant making $500K/year adds a website with online ordering:
- 18% increase from online ordering = **+$90,000/year**
- 25-30% savings on orders shifted from third-party to direct = **significant margin improvement**
- This means a $150-500/month website subscription pays for itself many times over

- Restaurant profit margins are **3-10%** -- large upfront costs are a barrier
- SaaS shifts cost from CapEx to OpEx, which restaurants prefer
- Subscription model creates **recurring revenue** and **long-term relationships**
- SaaS revenue growing at **19.38% CAGR** (2025-2029)
- The sweet spot for independent restaurants appears to be **$99-$299/month** based on competitor pricing
- Below $99/mo attracts tire-kickers; above $300/mo faces resistance from smaller operations

### Pricing Gap / Opportunity
- Generic builders (Wix, Squarespace) at $16-49/mo lack restaurant-specific features
- Restaurant platforms at $199-499/mo are expensive for small independents and food trucks
- **Clear gap in the $49-$149/month range** for a restaurant-focused solution with online ordering included

## 4. COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### BentoBox (owned by Fiserv)
- **Customers:** 14,000+ restaurants
- **Pricing:** Starting $199/mo + add-on fees
- **Strengths:** Beautiful custom designs, strong for upscale restaurants, good for dine-in/reservation focused, user-friendly CMS, solid onboarding support
- **Weaknesses:** No branded mobile app, limited customization (can't access source code, no plug-in marketplace), unreliable support reported, inventory sync problems, implementation/customization fees add up, not ideal for small operations
- **Best for:** Upscale, dine-in focused restaurants

### Popmenu
- **Customers:** 10,000+ restaurants
- **Pricing:** $179-$499/mo base + many add-ons
- **Strengths:** AI-driven marketing, social media features, data-driven campaigns, interactive menus
- **Weaknesses:** Online ordering NOT included in base price (adds ~$50/mo + $1/order), no custom mobile app, ordering redirects to different site (hurts SEO), costs rise quickly with usage, $300/mo per additional location, unpredictable costs at scale, $1,300+ onboarding fee
- **Best for:** Dine-in restaurants wanting social media and marketing tools

### Owner.com
- **Customers:** Growing rapidly (exact number not publicly stated)
- **Pricing:** $499/mo flat + 5% of orders
- **Strengths:** True all-in-one (website + app + ordering + loyalty + marketing), no upsells, branded mobile app (85% more repeat orders claimed), commission-free ordering (but 5% platform fee to customers)
- **Weaknesses:** Expensive at $499/mo especially for small restaurants, reported bugs and glitches, POS integration issues during peak times, limited customization, data privacy concerns in reviews, lock-in effects (3-month ramp-up period), customer order fees of $400-500/week reported by some users
- **Best for:** Mid-size independents wanting to move away from third-party platforms

### ChowNow
- **Customers:** Significant market presence
- **Pricing:** $119-$298/mo + processing fees + setup fees
- **Strengths:** Commission-free ordering, integrates with leading POS systems, branded website and mobile app, good for restaurants focused on direct ordering
- **Weaknesses:** Setup fees ($119-$499), annual developer fee for apps, $3.99/order delivery fee, less emphasis on website design quality
- **Best for:** Restaurants primarily wanting online ordering capability

### SpotHopper
- **Customers:** 18,000+ restaurants
- **Pricing:** Not publicly listed (estimate ~$100+/mo)
- **Strengths:** AI-powered marketing, large customer base, partnership with US Foods and Sysco for distribution, low time commitment (20-30 min/month)
- **Weaknesses:** Charges $2,000-$3,000 for photo/video ownership, pricing not transparent, limited public information
- **Best for:** Restaurants wanting hands-off marketing

### Generic Platforms (Wix, Squarespace)
- **Pricing:** $16-49/mo
- **Strengths:** Low cost, many templates, easy DIY
- **Weaknesses:** NOT restaurant-optimized, no POS integration, limited online ordering, no delivery management, no team management tools, no branded mobile apps, mobile layout issues (Wix), limited customization for restaurant needs, can't export sites easily, no loyalty programs, no restaurant-specific SEO
- **Best for:** Restaurants with zero budget wanting basic web presence

### Key Competitive Gaps
1. **No affordable all-in-one solution** -- either cheap-but-generic or expensive-but-restaurant-specific
2. **Food trucks and pop-ups are underserved** -- most platforms target sit-down restaurants
3. **Onboarding is painful** across competitors -- high setup fees and long ramp-up times
4. **True simplicity is missing** -- restaurant owners have 20-30 min/month for this; most platforms still require significant time investment
5. **Mobile apps** are either missing (BentoBox, Popmenu) or locked behind expensive tiers

## 5. FOOD TRUCK MARKET

### Market Size
- **92,257 food truck businesses** in the United States (IBISWorld 2025)
- Alternative count: ~48,000 food trucks (depends on definition)
- US food truck market size: **$2.8 billion** (IBISWorld 2026)
- Alternative US estimate: **$1.09 billion in 2025**, growing to **$1.59 billion by 2031**
- Global food truck market: **$4.71 billion** (2026), projected to reach **$6.46 billion by 2031**

### Growth Trends
- US food truck industry CAGR: **6.53%** (2026-2031)
- Industry has grown at a CAGR of **23.8%** in number of businesses between 2020-2025
- Revenue CAGR of **13.2%** over the past 5 years
- All-electric food trucks growing at **19.05% CAGR** through 2031
- Pop-up restaurant market: **$6.2 billion** (2024), growing at **8.4% CAGR** to $12.4 billion by 2033

### Digital Presence
- **86.9%** of food truck owners use Facebook
- **71.4%** use Instagram
- Only **22.6%** use TikTok
- **80.2% of food truck owners do NOT have a presence on food truck finder apps**
- **74% of customers discover food trucks through digital platforms**
- **70% of customers research a business online before visiting**
- **65% of food trucks** now partner with delivery apps
- **80% of operators** say consistent branding across digital and physical touchpoints builds trust

### Food Truck Website Gap
- No specific percentage found for food trucks with dedicated websites, but given that:
  - 28% of ALL small businesses lack websites
  - Food trucks are typically smaller/less resourced than restaurants
  - 80% don't even use food truck finder apps
  - Most rely heavily on social media (Facebook/Instagram) over owned digital properties
- **Estimated 40-60% of food trucks lack a dedicated website** (conservative inference based on available data)
- This represents **37,000 - 55,000 food trucks** as potential customers

### Platforms Currently Serving Food Trucks
- **Square Online** -- most commonly used due to simplicity and low cost
- **Toast** -- has mobile/food truck solutions
- **Social media** -- primary "platform" for most food trucks
- **Delivery apps** (Uber Eats, DoorDash) -- 65% partnership rate
- **Food truck finder apps** (StreetFoodFinder, Truckster) -- only 19.8% adoption
- **No dominant food-truck-specific website platform exists** -- this is a clear gap

## 6. PAIN POINTS

### Pain Points About Web Presence (or Lack Thereof)

**"I Don't Have Time"**
- Restaurant owners work 50-80 hour weeks
- They need someone else to manage their website -- they can spend 20-30 min/month max
- Day-to-day operations, health inspections, staffing consume all bandwidth

**"It's Too Expensive"**
- Many owners believe websites cost $5,000-$15,000+ (agency pricing mindset)
- 26% of small businesses without websites cite cost as primary factor
- Restaurant profit margins are 3-10% -- every dollar matters
- Third-party delivery commissions (15-30%) already eat into margins

**"I Don't Know How / It's Too Technical"**
- 35% of small businesses feel they are "too small" to need a website
- Owners excel at operations but struggle with marketing and technology
- Many tried DIY platforms and gave up or created poor-quality sites
- 33% of consumers discouraged by difficult-to-navigate restaurant websites

**"Social Media Is Enough"**
- 21% of SMBs rely solely on social media over a dedicated website
- 99% of full-service restaurants have social media vs only 69% with websites
- Reality: algorithms control social media reach; websites are owned real estate

**"My Existing Website Stinks But I'm Stuck"**
- 68% of diners have been DISCOURAGED from visiting because of a restaurant's website
- 33% discouraged by hard-to-navigate sites
- 30% discouraged by outdated-looking sites
- 45% look specifically for food photos; 36% discouraged by poor food photography
- Many restaurants have outdated, non-mobile-friendly sites they can't easily update

**"Third-Party Platforms Take Too Much"**
- Restaurants lose 25-30% of order value to DoorDash/UberEats/Grubhub
- True cost can exceed 40% including all fees
- But restaurants feel dependent -- 67% of consumers prefer ordering direct but can't if the restaurant doesn't offer it

**"I Can't Track What's Working"**
- Limited marketing budgets with no way to measure ROI
- 75% of restaurants offering more specials, 68% spending more on digital ads -- but many can't tell what's working
- Difficulty managing online reviews and reputation across platforms

## 7. SALES ANGLES

### Most Effective Messaging

**Lead with the Money:**
- "77% of your potential customers check your website before deciding to eat with you. If you don't have one, you're invisible to 3 out of 4 diners."
- "Restaurants with online ordering see an 18% increase in sales. On $500K revenue, that's $90,000 more per year."
- "You're paying DoorDash 25-30% commission. A direct ordering channel saves you 25-30 cents on every dollar."
- "The average digital order is 23% higher than walk-in orders."

**Lead with Fear of Loss:**
- "68% of diners have been turned away by a bad or missing restaurant website. How many customers are you losing right now?"
- "31% of consumers will not buy from a business without a website."
- "Your competitors who HAVE websites are getting your customers."

**Lead with Simplicity:**
- "You spend 20 minutes a month. We handle everything else."
- "We already built a preview of your site. Here's what it could look like." (show pre-built mockup)
- "You already have everything we need -- your menu, your photos, your story. We turn it into a website."

**Lead with ROI:**
- "For $X/month, you'll make that back with your first 2-3 online orders."
- "One order per day shifting from DoorDash to your own site saves you $200-300/month."

### Common Objections and Rebuttals

| Objection | Rebuttal |
|-----------|----------|
| "I'm too busy for this" | "That's exactly why we exist. Setup takes 30 minutes of your time. After that, 20 min/month. We handle the rest." |
| "It's too expensive" | "How much are you paying DoorDash? 25-30% of every order? Our service pays for itself by shifting just 1-2 orders/day to direct ordering." |
| "I have social media, that's enough" | "Social media is great for discovery, but 77% of diners check your website before they come in. And you can't take orders on Instagram." |
| "I tried a website before and it didn't work" | "Generic website builders aren't built for restaurants. We include online ordering, menu management, and Google optimization specifically for food businesses." |
| "My customers know where I am" | "70% of restaurant revenue comes from new or infrequent customers. They're Googling you right now. What do they find?" |
| "I'll think about it" | "I understand. Here's a preview of your site we already started. When you're ready, it takes 30 minutes to go live." |
| "I don't see the value" | "Restaurants with websites grow 2x faster. 18% more revenue from online ordering. And 67% of customers PREFER ordering directly from you -- but can't without a website." |

### Best Sales Tactics

1. **Show, don't tell:** Build a mockup/preview of their specific restaurant's website before approaching them. Selling a near-finished product is 10x easier than selling a concept.
2. **Go in person:** Door-to-door at restaurants (avoid peak hours: lunch 11-2, dinner 6-9). Everyone ignores emails and LinkedIn messages. Face-to-face is king.
3. **Keep it under 5 minutes:** Get the pitch to 4-5 minutes max. Restaurant owners don't have time.
4. **Research first:** Know their menu, their Yelp reviews, their Google listing. Reference specific things about THEIR business.
5. **Use their language:** Don't say "SEO" or "CMS." Say "more customers finding you on Google" and "you can update your menu from your phone."
6. **Timing matters:** Visit between 2-5 PM (after lunch rush, before dinner). Never during service.
7. **Target restaurants with problems:** Look for restaurants with no website, outdated websites, no online ordering, bad Google reviews, or only a Facebook page. These are the warmest leads.
8. **Social proof:** "We work with [X nearby restaurant] and they saw Y% more orders in their first month."

## KEY TAKEAWAYS & ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS

### The Opportunity is Real
- **142,500 - 232,500 restaurants** in the US without websites
- **~37,000 - 55,000 food trucks** without dedicated websites
- Growing pop-up restaurant market ($6.2B) largely unserved
- **58% of businesses without websites plan to get one in 2026** -- the market is ready

### The Pricing Sweet Spot
- Current competitors cluster at **$199-499/month**
- Generic tools are at **$16-49/month** but lack restaurant features
- **Clear gap at $49-$149/month** for restaurant-specific solution with online ordering included
- Monthly subscription model is strongly preferred over one-time payment in this industry

### The Strongest Value Proposition
- **Save money** from third-party delivery commissions (25-30% per order)
- **Make more money** with online ordering (+18% revenue, +23% higher order values)
- **Don't lose customers** who check websites before visiting (77% of diners)
- **Takes almost no time** (key differentiator -- these owners are maxed out)

### Underserved Segments
1. **Food trucks** -- 92,000+ businesses, no dominant platform, heavy social media users who need a simple ordering page
2. **Pop-up restaurants** -- $6.2B market growing at 8.4%, temporary operations need quick-launch websites
3. **Small independent restaurants** -- priced out of BentoBox/Owner.com/Popmenu, but need more than Wix/Squarespace
4. **Restaurants with outdated websites** -- 68% of diners have been turned away by bad websites; huge upgrade market
