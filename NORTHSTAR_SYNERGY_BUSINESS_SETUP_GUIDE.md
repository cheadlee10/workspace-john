## 1. BUSINESS FORMATION (WASHINGTON STATE)

### 1.1 LLC vs S-Corp vs C-Corp for a SaaS Business

**RECOMMENDATION: Start as a Washington LLC, then elect S-Corp tax treatment once net profit exceeds ~$60,000/year.**

| Factor | LLC (Default) | LLC with S-Corp Election | C-Corp |
|--------|---------------|--------------------------|--------|
| **Best for** | Early-stage, under $60K profit | $60K+ net profit, want to reduce self-employment tax | Seeking VC funding, reinvesting all profits |
| **Tax treatment** | Pass-through; all profits subject to self-employment tax (15.3%) | Pass-through; only "reasonable salary" portion subject to SE tax | Double taxation: 21% corporate rate + personal tax on dividends |
| **Complexity** | Low | Medium (payroll required) | High |
| **Flexibility** | High (flexible profit distribution) | Medium (strict IRS rules on "reasonable salary") | Low (rigid corporate formalities) |
| **WA-specific** | No state income tax, only B&O tax | Same B&O treatment, payroll tax applies | Same B&O treatment, more filings |

**Why LLC first:** Simplest to form, lowest compliance burden, and you can always elect S-Corp status later by filing IRS Form 2553. The IRS has been increasing S-Corp payroll audits, so you must set a legitimate "reasonable salary" if you elect S-Corp status.

**When to elect S-Corp:** Once your annual net profit consistently exceeds $60,000. At that point, the self-employment tax savings from splitting income between salary and distributions outweigh the added complexity of running payroll.

**When C-Corp makes sense:** Only if you plan to raise venture capital or want QSBS (Qualified Small Business Stock) tax exclusion benefits. For a bootstrapped restaurant SaaS, this is unlikely.

### 1.2 Washington State LLC Registration Process

**Step-by-step process:**

1. **Choose your LLC name** - Must include "Limited Liability Company," "LLC," or "L.L.C." Verify availability at the WA Secretary of State website.

2. **Appoint a Registered Agent** (see Section 1.6 below)

3. **File Certificate of Formation** with the Washington Secretary of State
   - **Online filing:** $200 (recommended - fastest)
   - **Mail filing:** $180
   - **Online processing time:** ~5 business days
   - **Mail processing time:** 3-4 weeks
   - **Expedited (3 business days):** Additional $100
   - **Same-day processing:** Additional $150

4. **File Initial Report** - Due within 120 days of formation
   - Free if filed simultaneously with Certificate of Formation
   - $10 fee if filed separately

5. **Ongoing: Annual Report** - $70/year to maintain active status

**Total startup cost (online):** $200 (formation) + $0 (initial report if filed simultaneously) = **$200 minimum**

### 1.3 EIN Application (IRS)

The Employer Identification Number is your business's federal tax ID. **This is free.**

**Steps:**
1. Form your LLC with Washington state FIRST (required before applying)
2. Go to IRS.gov > Apply for an Employer Identification Number
3. Complete the online application (available Mon-Fri, 7 AM - 10 PM Eastern)
4. **EIN is issued immediately upon submission**

**Alternative methods:**
- Fax (Form SS-4): ~4 business days
- Mail (Form SS-4): 4-5 weeks

**RECOMMENDATION:** Apply online immediately after your LLC formation is approved. Takes about 15 minutes.

### 1.4 Washington State Business License

Washington requires a state-level business license for nearly all businesses.

**Process:**
1. Go to the Washington Department of Revenue (DOR) website
2. Apply online via "My DOR eServices" at https://dor.wa.gov/open-business/apply-business-license
3. You'll receive:
   - A Unified Business Identifier (UBI) number
   - Your state business license
   - Registration for applicable state taxes (B&O, sales tax)
   - Your filing frequency assignment (monthly, quarterly, or annual)

**Cost:** The state business license application itself is part of the registration process through DOR. The specific fee varies but is nominal.

**Timeline:** Processing typically takes 1-2 weeks.

### 1.5 City of Woodinville / King County Business License

**Woodinville City Business License:**
- **Annual cost:** $39/year
- **Required for:** All businesses with a physical location in city limits, OR businesses outside the city generating over $2,000 in annual gross income within Woodinville
- **Application:** Through the Washington State DOR business license application - select the Woodinville city endorsement
- **Home-based businesses:** Require an additional Home Business Permit Application, emailed to the Development Services Department
  - Level 1 Home Business Permit: No additional cost
  - Level 2 Home Business Permit: Additional fee applies
- **Exemptions:** 501(c)(3) nonprofits may be exempt from city fees
- **Contact:** Development Services at 425-489-2700

**King County:**
- King County does not impose a separate general business license for businesses within incorporated cities like Woodinville
- Businesses in unincorporated King County may need a separate county license

**FileLocal Portal:**
- Many Puget Sound area cities use the FileLocal platform for city business license management
- Check if Woodinville uses FileLocal; if so, create a separate FileLocal account after getting your state UBI number
- Two-step process: State license first via MyDOR, then city license via FileLocal

### 1.6 Registered Agent Requirements

**Legal requirement:** Every Washington LLC must maintain a Registered Agent with a physical Washington state address at all times.

**Who can be a Registered Agent:**
- You (the owner) - as long as you have a physical WA address (not PO Box) and are available during regular business hours
- A professional registered agent service

**Registered Agent duties:**
- Accept legal mail and service of process on behalf of your LLC
- Receive annual report reminders and official correspondence from the Secretary of State
- Forward all received documents to the LLC

**Options and costs:**
| Option | Cost | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|------|
| Be your own agent | Free | No cost | Your home address becomes public record; must be available during business hours |
| Northwest Registered Agent | ~$125/year | Privacy protection, reliable | Annual cost |
| Other services (LegalZoom, etc.) | $50-$300/year | Various service levels | Varies |

**RECOMMENDATION:** If you work from home and don't mind your address being public, be your own agent. Otherwise, use Northwest Registered Agent ($125/year) for privacy.

**Changing agents:** Free to file a Statement of Change; $50 for expedited processing.

### 1.7 Operating Agreement Basics

**Legal requirement:** Washington State does NOT require a written operating agreement, but you should absolutely have one. It's an internal document - you don't file it with the state.

**What to include:**

1. **Company Information**
   - Full legal name: NorthStar Synergy LLC
   - Principal business address
   - Formation date
   - Registered agent name and address
   - LLC's expected lifespan (typically "perpetual")

2. **Membership/Ownership**
   - Full legal names and addresses of all members
   - Ownership percentages
   - Capital contributions (initial investment amounts)

3. **Management Structure**
   - Member-managed (all members participate in decisions) vs. Manager-managed (designated managers handle operations)
   - For a small startup: member-managed is typical

4. **Financial Provisions**
   - How profits and losses are distributed
   - Distribution schedule (monthly, quarterly, annual)
   - How capital calls work if more funding is needed
   - Banking authority (who can sign checks, access accounts)

5. **Voting and Decision-Making**
   - What requires unanimous consent vs. majority vote
   - Procedures for major decisions (taking on debt, adding members, selling the company)

6. **Membership Changes**
   - How new members can be admitted
   - How existing members can sell or transfer their interest
   - Right of first refusal for other members
   - What happens if a member dies, becomes disabled, or wants to leave

7. **Dissolution Provisions**
   - Conditions that trigger dissolution
   - How assets are distributed upon dissolution

**RECOMMENDATION:** Use a template from Northwest Registered Agent or LegalZoom as a starting point, or have an attorney draft one ($500-$1,500). For a single-member LLC, a simpler agreement suffices. Multi-member LLCs need a thorough agreement.

## 2. WASHINGTON STATE TAXES

### 2.1 B&O (Business & Occupation) Tax

Washington's B&O tax is a **gross receipts tax** - it applies to gross revenue, NOT profit. There are no deductions for costs of doing business.

**Current Rates (effective October 1, 2025):**

The B&O rate for "Service and Other Activities" (which covers SaaS) is tiered based on prior calendar year gross income:

| Annual Gross Income | B&O Tax Rate |
|---------------------|-------------|
| Less than $1 million | 1.5% |
| $1 million - $4,999,999 | 1.75% |
| $5 million or more | 2.1% |

**For NorthStar Synergy starting out:** You'll be at the 1.5% rate.

**Example:** If you earn $100,000 in gross revenue, your B&O tax = $1,500.

**Filing Frequency:**
- **Annual filing:** For very small businesses (under ~$28,000/year) - due April 15
- **Quarterly filing:** For moderate-revenue businesses - due the last day of the month following the quarter:
  - Q1 (Jan-Mar): Due April 30
  - Q2 (Apr-Jun): Due July 31
  - Q3 (Jul-Sep): Due October 31
  - Q4 (Oct-Dec): Due January 31
- **Monthly filing:** For higher-revenue businesses - due the 25th of the following month

The DOR assigns your filing frequency when you register. You can request a change if your revenue changes significantly.

**Small Business B&O Tax Credit:** Businesses with annual taxable income under $125,000 may qualify for a reduced B&O tax obligation through the small business credit. The credit gradually phases out between $62,500 and $125,000 in annual revenue.

**Where to file:** My DOR eServices portal at https://dor.wa.gov

### 2.2 Sales Tax on SaaS in Washington State

**YES, SaaS is taxable in Washington.** Washington classifies SaaS as "digital automated services," making it subject to retail sales tax.

**Combined Sales Tax Rate for Woodinville: 10.3%**
- Washington state rate: 6.5%
- Local (Woodinville/King County) rate: 3.8%

**What this means for NorthStar Synergy:** You must charge your Washington-based restaurant customers 10.3% sales tax on top of your subscription price. The exact rate depends on the customer's location (destination-based sourcing).

**Important October 2025 expansion:** As of October 1, 2025, Washington expanded retail sales tax to cover additional IT services including custom website development, custom software development, IT support and training, and software customization. This reinforces that your restaurant website/ordering platform subscriptions are taxable.

### 2.3 How to Collect and Remit Sales Tax

**Registration:**
1. Register for a sales tax account through MyDOR when you apply for your business license
2. You'll receive a tax account ID and filing frequency assignment

**Collection:**
- Charge the appropriate combined sales tax rate based on the customer's location (destination-based)
- Use tools like Avalara, TaxJar, or Stripe Tax to automate rate lookups
- Include sales tax as a separate line item on invoices

**Remittance:**
- File and pay through the My DOR eServices portal
- Same filing frequency as your B&O tax (monthly, quarterly, or annual)
- Same due dates as B&O tax

**No small business exemption** for collecting sales tax - if you make taxable sales in Washington, you must collect and remit regardless of revenue size.

### 2.4 Out-of-State Sales Tax / Nexus Rules

**Do you need to charge sales tax to out-of-state restaurant customers?**

This depends on TWO factors for each state:
1. **Do you have nexus (a tax obligation) in that state?**
2. **Is SaaS taxable in that state?**

**Economic Nexus Thresholds (most common):**
- $100,000 in sales OR 200 separate transactions into a state within a 12-month period
- Some states only use the $100,000 revenue threshold

**SaaS taxability varies by state. Key examples:**
| State | SaaS Taxable? | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| Washington | YES | 10.3% in Woodinville |
| California | NO | Generally not taxable |
| Texas | YES | Taxable as data processing |
| New York | YES | Taxable as prewritten software |
| Oregon | NO (no sales tax) | Oregon has no sales tax |
| Idaho | NO | Generally not taxable |

**In 2026, SaaS is taxable in approximately 25 US jurisdictions.**

**RECOMMENDATION for NorthStar Synergy:**
- Initially, most restaurant customers will likely be in Washington state - collect WA sales tax from day one
- Once you exceed $100,000 in sales to any individual state, research that state's SaaS taxability
- Use an automated sales tax tool (Avalara, TaxJar, or Stripe Tax) to manage multi-state compliance as you grow
- Consider consulting a tax professional when you start selling to more than 2-3 states

### 2.5 Quarterly Estimated Federal Tax Payments

Since Washington has no state income tax, your quarterly estimated payments are **federal only.**

**Who must pay:** If you expect to owe $1,000+ in federal taxes for the year, the IRS requires quarterly estimated payments.

**2026 Due Dates:**
| Period | Due Date |
|--------|----------|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar 2026) | April 15, 2026 |
| Q2 (Apr-May 2026) | June 16, 2026 |
| Q3 (Jun-Aug 2026) | September 15, 2026 |
| Q4 (Sep-Dec 2026) | January 15, 2027 |

**How to calculate:** Use IRS Form 1040-ES. Safe harbor: pay either 100% of last year's tax liability or 90% of current year's expected liability (110% of last year if AGI > $150,000).

**How to pay:** IRS Direct Pay (irs.gov/payments), EFTPS (Electronic Federal Tax Payment System), or by mail with Form 1040-ES voucher.

**As an LLC (default):** You'll pay self-employment tax (15.3% on first ~$168,600 of net earnings, then 2.9% on the rest) PLUS regular income tax on all net business income.

### 2.6 Annual Filing Requirements Summary

| Filing | Due Date | Where |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Federal Income Tax (Form 1040 + Schedule C/SE for sole proprietor LLC) | April 15 | IRS |
| WA B&O Tax | Per assigned frequency | MyDOR |
| WA Sales Tax | Per assigned frequency | MyDOR |

## 3. PAYMENT PROCESSING FOR SAAS

### 3.1 Stripe Billing for Recurring Subscriptions

**RECOMMENDATION: Use Stripe Billing.** It's the industry standard for SaaS subscription management.

**Setup Process:**
1. Create a Stripe account at stripe.com
2. Complete business verification (EIN, business address, bank account)
3. Set up your Products and Prices in the Stripe Dashboard
4. Choose integration method:
   - **Stripe Checkout** (hosted payment page - fastest to implement)
   - **Stripe Elements** (embedded in your website - more customization)
   - **Customer Portal** (let customers self-manage subscriptions)
5. Configure Billing settings (retry logic, dunning emails, etc.)

**Key Features:**
- Recurring billing (monthly, quarterly, annual)
- Multiple pricing models: flat-rate, per-seat, tiered, metered/usage-based
- Built-in customer portal for subscription self-management
- Automatic invoice generation and receipt emails
- Revenue recognition
- Tax calculation (Stripe Tax add-on)
- Trials, discounts, coupons, and promotional pricing

### 3.2 Stripe Pricing Breakdown

| Fee Type | Cost |
|----------|------|
| **Stripe Billing** (subscription management) | 0.7% of billing volume |
| **Card payment processing** | 2.9% + $0.30 per successful charge |
| **ACH Direct Debit** | 0.8% per transaction (capped at $5) |
| **Stripe Invoicing** (one-time invoices) | ~$0.50 per invoice (Starter) |
| **Stripe Tax** (automatic tax calculation) | 0.5% per transaction |

**Total cost example for a $200/month subscription (credit card):**
- Payment processing: $200 x 2.9% + $0.30 = $6.10
- Billing fee: $200 x 0.7% = $1.40
- **Total Stripe fees: $7.50 per $200 charge (3.75%)**

**With ACH instead of credit card:**
- ACH processing: $200 x 0.8% = $1.60 (vs $6.10 for cards)
- Billing fee: $200 x 0.7% = $1.40
- **Total Stripe fees: $3.00 per $200 charge (1.5%)**

### 3.3 Failed Payment Handling / Dunning

Stripe provides built-in dunning tools at no extra charge:

**Smart Retries:**
- Stripe uses machine learning to analyze historical payment data and determine the optimal time to retry failed payments
- Recovers an average of **41% of failed recurring payments**
- Smart retries recover **14% more revenue** than fixed-schedule retries

**Dunning Email Sequence:**
- Automated customer notifications when a payment fails
- Configurable reminder intervals (e.g., day 1, day 3, day 7)
- Customizable email templates
- Multiple communication channels (email, SMS)

**Configuration options:**
- Number of retry attempts before canceling the subscription
- Grace period before marking subscription as past due
- Whether to prorate on recovery
- Custom webhook events for failed payments (for your own notifications)

**ACH Direct Debit note:** Stripe does not automatically retry failed ACH payments by default (unlike card payments).

### 3.4 Receipt Emails

Stripe automatically sends receipt emails to customers after successful payments. You can:
- Customize receipt email branding (logo, colors, footer)
- Enable/disable automatic receipts
- Include custom fields or messaging
- Provide downloadable PDF receipts

### 3.5 Upgrades/Downgrades Mid-Cycle

Stripe handles proration automatically:

**Upgrade mid-cycle:** Customer is immediately charged the prorated difference for the remainder of the billing period, then billed at the new rate next cycle.

**Downgrade mid-cycle:** Credit is applied to the customer's account for the prorated unused portion, reducing their next invoice.

**Configuration options:**
- `proration_behavior`: `create_prorations` (default), `none`, or `always_invoice`
- You can preview proration amounts before confirming changes
- The customer portal can allow self-service plan changes

### 3.6 ACH vs Credit Card for B2B Subscriptions

| Factor | Credit Card | ACH Direct Debit |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| **Cost to you** | 2.9% + $0.30 | 0.8% (max $5) |
| **Cost on $200 charge** | $6.10 | $1.60 |
| **Cost on $500 charge** | $14.80 | $4.00 |
| **Settlement time** | 2 business days | 4-5 business days |
| **Failure rate** | Lower (instant validation) | Higher (can fail days later) |
| **Customer convenience** | High (everyone has cards) | Medium (requires bank details) |
| **Chargebacks** | Possible | Rare |

**RECOMMENDATION:** Offer both. Default to credit card for ease of onboarding, but encourage ACH for customers on higher-tier plans (the savings add up). For a $300/month plan, ACH saves you ~$130/year per customer.

### 3.7 PCI Compliance Requirements

**Good news:** Using Stripe handles most PCI compliance for you.

- Stripe is a **PCI Level 1 Service Provider** (the highest certification level)
- Stripe also maintains SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 certifications
- As long as you use Stripe Checkout or Stripe Elements, **you never handle raw card data**

**Your responsibilities (PCI SAQ A or SAQ A-EP):**
- Use HTTPS on your website
- Don't store, process, or transmit cardholder data on your servers
- Use Stripe.js or Stripe Checkout for payment collection
- Complete the annual PCI Self-Assessment Questionnaire (SAQ A - simplest level)
- Keep your integration updated

**Noncompliance penalties:** $5,000-$100,000/month, and potential loss of ability to process card payments.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Use Stripe Checkout (hosted) initially. It's the simplest path to PCI compliance and requires only SAQ A (the shortest questionnaire).

## 4. INVOICING & RECEIPTS

### 4.1 Legal Requirements for Invoices

The US has **no federal invoicing requirements** (unlike the EU). However, best practice and state-level expectations mean invoices should include:

**Required/Expected elements:**
1. **Your business information:** NorthStar Synergy LLC, address, phone, email
2. **Customer information:** Business name, address, contact
3. **Unique invoice number** (sequential recommended, e.g., INV-2026-001)
4. **Invoice date** and **due date**
5. **Itemized description** of services (e.g., "Monthly Website & Ordering Platform Subscription - Pro Plan")
6. **Subscription period** covered (e.g., "March 1-31, 2026")
7. **Subtotal**
8. **Sales tax** (listed separately with rate and amount)
9. **Total amount due**
10. **Payment terms** (e.g., "Due upon receipt" or "Net 15")
11. **Payment methods accepted**
12. **Your EIN** (optional but professional for B2B)

### 4.2 Automated Invoice Generation

**Stripe Billing handles this automatically:**
- Invoices generated at each billing cycle
- Sent to customers via email with a hosted payment link
- PDF download available
- Customizable with your logo, colors, and custom fields
- Includes all legally recommended elements
- Automatic numbering

### 4.4 Annual Prepayment Invoicing

For customers who prepay annually (common in SaaS - often offered at a discount):

**Best practice:**
1. Create a single invoice for the full annual amount
2. Clearly state the service period (e.g., "March 2026 - February 2027")
3. Apply any annual discount (typically 10-20% off monthly rate)
4. Revenue should be **recognized monthly** in your accounting (deferred revenue), not all at once

**In Stripe:**
- Set up an annual price in your product catalog
- Stripe automatically generates a single invoice for the annual amount
- Use Stripe Revenue Recognition to properly defer and recognize revenue monthly

**In QuickBooks/Xero:**
- Record the payment as deferred revenue (liability)
- Create a monthly journal entry moving 1/12 to recognized revenue
- This is important for accurate monthly P&L statements

### 5.4 MRR/ARR Tracking

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** = Total number of active subscriptions x average revenue per account

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** = MRR x 12

**MRR Components to Track:**

| Component | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|
| **New MRR** | Revenue from brand new customers this month |
| **Expansion MRR** | Additional revenue from existing customers (upgrades, add-ons) |
| **Reactivation MRR** | Revenue from customers who previously churned and returned |
| **Contraction MRR** | Revenue lost from existing customers (downgrades) |
| **Churned MRR** | Revenue lost from customers who cancelled |
| **Net New MRR** | New + Expansion + Reactivation - Contraction - Churned |

**Important:** Do NOT include one-time fees (setup, custom development) in MRR/ARR calculations. Only recurring subscription revenue.

### 5.7 When Do You Need an Actual Accountant vs. Self-Managed?

**Self-manage (with QuickBooks) when:**
- Revenue under ~$50K/year
- Simple, predictable subscription revenue
- Single-member LLC with straightforward finances
- You're comfortable with basic bookkeeping

**Hire a bookkeeper ($200-$500/month) when:**
- Transaction volume exceeds what you can review weekly
- You're spending more than 2-3 hours per week on books
- Revenue exceeds ~$50K/year
- **Critical:** Make sure they understand SaaS accounting (deferred revenue, MRR recognition)

**Hire a CPA when:**
- Annual tax filing (even if you self-manage books, a CPA for annual taxes is wise from day one)
- Considering S-Corp election
- Multi-state tax obligations
- Revenue exceeds ~$100K/year
- You need tax planning advice

**Key warning:** Among SaaS companies under $15M ARR, 90% outsource their bookkeeping. The biggest risk with non-SaaS bookkeepers is errors in revenue recognition and deferred revenue handling. Hire someone who understands subscriptions.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Self-manage with QuickBooks + Stripe sync for the first year. Hire a CPA for annual tax filing from year one ($500-$2,000). Add a bookkeeper once revenue is consistent and your time is better spent on sales/product.

### 6.3 Service Level Agreement (SLA)

**Industry benchmark:** 99.9% guaranteed uptime (allows ~8.7 hours downtime per year)

### 6.5 Cancellation/Refund Policy Best Practices

**RECOMMENDATION for NorthStar Synergy:**

**Cancellation Policy:**
- Monthly plans: Cancel anytime, effective at end of current billing period. No partial-month refunds.
- Annual plans: Cancel anytime, service continues through the end of the prepaid period. No prorated refund on annual plans (or optionally, offer prorated refunds minus any discounts received).
- Require 30 days written notice for cancellation
- Provide a 30-day data export window after cancellation before data deletion

**Refund Policy:**
- Offer a 14-day or 30-day money-back guarantee for new customers (builds trust, reduces perceived risk)
- After the guarantee period: no refunds for unused time
- Billing errors: refund promptly
- Be clear and specific - avoid "case-by-case" language

**Display your policy:**
- In the Terms of Service
- On your pricing page
- At checkout
- In the website footer

### 6.6 Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**When you need one:** Since NorthStar Synergy will process restaurant customers' personal data (diners' names, emails, phone numbers, order history, payment information), you need a DPA.

**Key DPA components:**

1. **Definitions** - What constitutes PII, what privacy regulations apply (CCPA, state laws)
2. **Processing scope** - What data you process, for what purpose, for how long
3. **Data categories** - Names, emails, phone numbers, addresses, order history, payment metadata
4. **Data subjects** - Restaurant customers (diners)
5. **Security requirements** - Technical, administrative, and physical safeguards
6. **Subprocessor management** - List of third parties who also process data (Stripe, hosting provider, email service)
7. **Breach notification** - Timeline and process for notifying affected parties (typically within 72 hours)
8. **Data deletion** - What happens to data when the contract ends
9. **Audit rights** - Customer's right to verify your compliance

**RECOMMENDATION:** Draft a standard DPA template and include it as an addendum to your service agreement. Use templates from GetTerms.io or Juro as a starting point.

### 6.7 CCPA / State Privacy Law Compliance

**CCPA Applicability (2026 thresholds):**
The CCPA applies if your business meets ANY of these:
- Annual gross revenue exceeds $26,625,000 (adjusted for inflation)
- Process personal information of 100,000+ California residents/households annually
- Derive 50%+ of annual revenue from selling/sharing personal information

**For NorthStar Synergy starting out:** You likely won't meet CCPA thresholds initially. However, build privacy-compliant practices from day one because:
1. Washington state has its own privacy protections
2. Other state laws may apply based on where your restaurant customers' diners are located
3. It's much easier to start right than to retrofit compliance later

**2026 CCPA changes to be aware of:**
- Opt-out confirmations are now mandatory (not optional)
- Businesses over $25M may need formal cybersecurity audits
- Automated decision-making technology has new requirements

**Practical steps regardless of CCPA threshold:**
1. Maintain a clear, comprehensive Privacy Policy
2. Provide a way for consumers to request data access, deletion, and correction
3. Keep records of consumer requests for at least 24 months
4. Offer at least two methods for submitting privacy requests (e.g., email + web form)
5. Don't sell personal data without consent
6. Sign DPAs with all subprocessors

## 9. DASHBOARD / REPORTING

### 9.1 What Financial Dashboards Should the Business Have?

**Dashboard 1: Revenue & Growth (weekly/daily review)**
- MRR (current and trend)
- MRR breakdown: New, Expansion, Contraction, Churned, Reactivation
- ARR
- Revenue growth rate (month-over-month)
- Number of active subscriptions
- Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA)

**Dashboard 2: Customer Economics (monthly review)**
- Customer Lifetime Value (LTV): Average revenue per customer x average customer lifespan
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): Total sales + marketing spend / new customers acquired
- LTV:CAC Ratio (target 3:1 or higher)
- CAC Payback Period: CAC / monthly revenue per customer (target 5-12 months)

**Dashboard 3: Customer Health (weekly review)**
- Churn rate: Monthly and annual (target under 5% monthly for SMB SaaS)
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR): Target over 100% (means expansion revenue offsets churn)
- Customer retention rate: Target 85-90%+

**Dashboard 4: Financial Operations (monthly review)**
- Monthly P&L summary
- Gross margin (target 70%+)
- Operating expenses by category
- Cash balance and runway
- Accounts receivable aging
- Rule of 40: Revenue growth rate + profit margin (target 40%+)

### 9.2 MRR, ARR, Churn Rate, LTV, CAC Formulas

**MRR** = Sum of all active subscription amounts for the month
- Example: 20 restaurants at $200/mo + 5 at $350/mo = $5,750 MRR

**ARR** = MRR x 12
- Example: $5,750 x 12 = $69,000 ARR

**Monthly Churn Rate** = (Customers lost this month / Customers at start of month) x 100
- Example: Lost 1 out of 25 = 4% monthly churn

**Revenue Churn Rate** = (MRR lost this month / MRR at start of month) x 100

**Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** = ((Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR) x 100
- Target: 100%+ (over 120% is excellent)

**Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)** = ARPA / Monthly Churn Rate
- Example: $230 ARPA / 0.04 churn = $5,750 LTV

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** = Total Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired
- Example: $2,000 spent / 5 new customers = $400 CAC

**LTV:CAC Ratio** = LTV / CAC
- Example: $5,750 / $400 = 14.4:1 (excellent)
- Target: At least 3:1

### 9.4 Building a Custom Dashboard

**Yes, you can build a custom dashboard.** Options:

**Option A: Spreadsheet Dashboard (Free, manual)**
- Google Sheets or Excel with monthly manual data entry
- Works fine for the first 6-12 months
- Create tabs for MRR tracking, customer list, P&L, and key metrics
- Formulas calculate all metrics automatically
- Downside: Manual data entry, no real-time updates

**Option B: Google Looker Studio + Stripe Data (Free tool, some setup)**
- Connect Stripe data to Google Sheets via Zapier or a Stripe-to-Sheets integration
- Build visual dashboards in Looker Studio (formerly Google Data Studio)
- Semi-automated with periodic data syncs
- Good for custom views and sharing

**Option C: Custom Code Dashboard (Development investment)**
- Build a custom dashboard using your existing tech stack
- Pull data from the Stripe API (subscriptions, invoices, customers)
- Use a charting library (Chart.js, Recharts, D3.js)
- Full control over metrics, calculations, and visualizations
- Can integrate with your own CRM/admin panel
- Most flexible but highest development cost

**RECOMMENDATION:** Start with ChartMogul (free) for SaaS metrics + QuickBooks reports for financial data. Build a custom dashboard only if you have very specific needs that off-the-shelf tools don't meet, or once you want to consolidate SaaS metrics + operational data + financial data into a single view.
