## 5 Cold Email Templates

### Cold Email 1 — "Quick Win" Pattern
**Subject options:**
- Quick idea for {company}
- {first_name}, small revenue leak I noticed
- 2-minute idea for {company}

**Email body:**
Hi {first_name},

I took a quick look at {company} and noticed a likely opportunity around {pain_point}. Most teams in {industry} lose qualified leads here without realizing it.

At NorthStar Synergy, we build simple automation + follow-up systems that recover missed opportunities and increase booked calls without adding headcount.

If useful, I can send a 3-point teardown of what I’d fix first for {company}.

**CTA:** Open to a quick 12-minute call this week, or should I just email the teardown?

## 5 Follow-Up Email Variants

### Follow-Up 1 — Gentle Bump (2–3 days)
**Subject options:**
- Re: quick idea for {company}
- Bumping this, {first_name}
- Worth a look?

**Email body:**
Hi {first_name},

Wanted to bump this in case it got buried.

Still happy to share a quick teardown of where {company} could improve {pain_point} and capture more revenue from current lead flow.

**CTA:** Want me to send the 3-point version here?

---

### Follow-Up 2 — Value Add (3–5 days)
**Subject options:**
- 3 ideas for {company}
- Quick wins I’d test first
- Practical improvements for {industry} ops

**Email body:**
Hi {first_name},

To make this useful, here are 3 quick wins we often implement first:
1) instant lead acknowledgment,
2) timed follow-up sequence for no-replies,
3) simple "stalled deal" alerting.

These alone often improve response consistency and close-rate velocity.

**CTA:** If helpful, I can map these directly to {company} in a short call.

---

### Follow-Up 3 — Social Proof Style (5–7 days)
**Subject options:**
- Similar teams had this same issue
- Why this tends to work fast
- Seen this in multiple {industry} teams

**Email body:**
Hi {first_name},

We regularly hear: "Our team is busy, but follow-up still slips."

Once we implement automated first-touch + structured re-engagement, teams typically see cleaner pipelines and fewer missed opportunities within weeks.

Could be relevant if {company} is balancing growth and team bandwidth.

**CTA:** Open to a quick fit check this week?

---

### Follow-Up 4 — Breakup (7–10 days)

## Tone Notes for Higher Reply Rates
- Keep lines short and skimmable.
- Use one clear CTA per message.
- Ask low-friction questions ("want me to send…?").
- Personalize first 2 lines minimum.
- Avoid jargon and long credentials section.
