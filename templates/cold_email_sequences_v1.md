## Quick Reference

| Sequence | Target | Use When |
|----------|--------|----------|
| A | No Website | Business has GMB/Yelp but no website |
| B | Poor Google Rank | Has website but buried on page 2+ |
| C | Outdated Site | Website exists but old/slow/broken |

**Personalization Variables:**
- `{{FirstName}}` - Decision maker's first name
- `{{CompanyName}}` - Business name (match their branding exactly)
- `{{City}}` - Primary service area
- `{{Service}}` - Their main service (e.g., "plumbing", "landscaping")
- `{{ReviewCount}}` - Number of Google/Yelp reviews
- `{{ReviewSource}}` - Where you found reviews (Google, Yelp, Facebook)
- `{{GooglePage}}` - What page they appear on for "[service] [city]"
- `{{SiteIssue}}` - Specific problem (slow load time, not mobile-friendly, etc.)
- `{{SimilarBiz}}` - Comparable business we helped
- `{{SimilarCity}}` - City of that business
- `{{Result}}` - Specific result (e.g., "12 new leads in 30 days")
- `{{PreviewLink}}` - URL to screenshot/preview mockup

## CTA VARIANTS

Use these interchangeably to A/B test:

**Soft (Lowest friction - recommended for cold):**
- Worth a look?
- Worth a quick look?
- Worth a conversation?
- Mind if I send a 2-minute walkthrough?
- Curious what you think.

**Medium:**
- Want me to walk you through it?
- Happy to show you -- takes 2 minutes.
- Open to a quick chat this week?

**Direct (Use only after engagement):**
- Can we do a 10-minute call Tuesday or Wednesday?
- Reply "YES" and I will send booking link.

## BREAKUP EMAIL (Day 17 - Optional)

**Use for all sequences when no response after Follow-Up 2**

**Subject:** `{{CompanyName}} -- closing the loop`

**Email Body:**

```
Hi {{FirstName}},

I have reached out a couple of times about helping {{CompanyName}} get more customers from Google and have not heard back -- totally understand, you are busy running a business.

I will stop reaching out after this. But if you ever want to explore it, the offer stands. Just reply to this email anytime.

Wishing you a great rest of the year.

John
NorthStar Synergy
```

**Word count:** ~65

**Why this works:** Breakup emails consistently generate surprising reply rates by removing pressure and triggering loss aversion.

## SUBJECT LINE A/B TEST PRIORITY

**Tier 1 - Test First (Highest open rates):**
- Question format: `Quick question about {{CompanyName}}`
- Short + Company: `Idea for {{CompanyName}}`

**Tier 2 - Test Second:**
- Problem hint: `Missing calls in {{City}}?`
- Personalized: `{{FirstName}}, quick thought on your website?`

**Tier 3 - Rotate In:**
- Specific: `{{CompanyName}} + Google visibility`
- Direct: `{{CompanyName}} website -- quick observation`

## PRE-SEND CHECKLIST

Before sending ANY email from these templates:

- [ ] ALL {{variables}} replaced with real, verified data
- [ ] Company name spelled correctly and matches their branding
- [ ] First name confirmed (check LinkedIn, GMB, website)
- [ ] {{PreviewLink}} is live and clickable
- [ ] Pasted through plain-text editor (no smart quotes)
- [ ] No "?" where apostrophes should be
- [ ] Under 100 words (body only)
- [ ] No pricing mentioned
- [ ] Soft CTA only
- [ ] Follow-ups scheduled (Day 3, Day 10)
- [ ] Sent Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am local time

## METRICS TO TRACK

| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Open Rate | 40%+ | Below 25% |
| Reply Rate | 7%+ | Below 3% |
| Meeting Rate | 2%+ | Below 0.5% |
| Unsubscribe Rate | Below 1% | Above 3% |

If open rate drops: Test new subject lines.  
If reply rate drops: Check personalization quality and CTA.  
If unsubscribe spikes: Reduce frequency or improve targeting.
