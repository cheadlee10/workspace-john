## 8) Simple Demo Flow Script (3–5 minutes)

Use this script during calls, screen recordings, or landing page interactive demos.

### Demo Title
**From manual chaos to automated flow in minutes**

### Demo Setup (On Screen)
- A sample intake form
- A live spreadsheet/dashboard
- A task board or follow-up queue
- Notification channel (email/Slack placeholder)

### Script

**Step 1 — Problem Snapshot (20–30 sec)**  
“Here’s the current state: new requests come in, someone copies data into a sheet, then manually updates status and sends follow-ups. It works—but it leaks time and creates errors.”

**Step 2 — Trigger Event (30–40 sec)**  
“I’ll submit one sample request now. This is the same entry point your team already uses.”

**Step 3 — Automation in Motion (60–90 sec)**  
“As soon as it’s submitted, watch what happens automatically: data is validated, logged to the tracker, categorized by priority, and routed to the right owner. No copy/paste.”

**Step 4 — Visibility & Control (45–60 sec)**  
“Now we open the dashboard. You can see status, owner, and turnaround metrics in one place. If anything is overdue, the system flags it automatically.”

**Step 5 — Business Outcome (30–45 sec)**  
“This is where teams save time immediately—fewer manual touches, faster response, cleaner reporting, and less rework.”

**Step 6 — Close (15–20 sec)**  
“We can deploy a version like this around your exact process in days, then optimize from real usage.”

### Demo Presenter Notes
- Keep language outcome-focused: time saved, speed gained, error reduced
- Avoid technical jargon unless the buyer asks
- Tie every feature to a business result
- End with one clear next step: **book workflow audit**
