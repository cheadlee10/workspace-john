## CORE FRAMEWORKS (Research-Backed)

### Framework 1: LAER (Industry Standard)

**L**isten — Hear the full objection without interrupting
**A**cknowledge — Validate their concern (never dismiss)
**E**xplore — Ask questions to understand the root cause
**R**espond — Address the real issue, not surface objection

**Why it works:** Most objections are surface-level. Exploring reveals the real concern.

**Example:**
```
Prospect: "It's too expensive."

❌ BAD: "Actually, $399 is very affordable."
✅ GOOD:
  Listen: [Let them finish]
  Acknowledge: "I hear you — budget is important."
  Explore: "Can I ask — is it the total cost, or is it about not seeing the ROI yet?"
  Respond: [Address their REAL concern — usually ROI, not price]
```

### Framework 2: Feel-Felt-Found (Empathy Bridge)

**Feel** — "I understand how you feel..."
**Felt** — "Other [landscapers/contractors] felt the same way..."
**Found** — "But what they found was..."

**Why it works:** Creates peer social proof + empathy + resolution

**Example:**
```
Prospect: "I don't have time to deal with this right now."

Response:
"I totally understand how you feel. You're running a business and every minute counts.

A lot of landscapers I've worked with felt the same way initially — they thought building a website would be this huge time sink.

But what they found was that it actually took 30 minutes total from them, and then it started bringing in 3-5 extra customers per month WITHOUT any extra effort. One guy told me it was the best 30 minutes he ever spent.

Does that make sense?"
```

### Framework 3: Assumptive Close (Move Forward)

**Technique:** Assume the sale, address objection as implementation detail

**Example:**
```
Prospect: "What if I don't like what you build?"

❌ BAD: "Well, you can always not use it..."
✅ GOOD: "Great question. Here's how we'll handle that: I'll send you a preview before it goes live. You tell me what to change, I change it within 24 hours. We go back and forth until you love it. Then we launch. Sound fair?"
```

## TOP 15 OBJECTIONS + RESPONSES

### 1. "IT'S TOO EXPENSIVE" (Price Objection)

**Root Cause:** Usually NOT about absolute price — it's about perceived value vs cost

**LAER Response:**
```
Listen: [full objection]
Acknowledge: "I hear you — $399 is real money."
Explore: "Can I ask — is it the total amount, or are you unsure about the ROI?"

[If ROI]
Respond: "Got it. Let me show you the math:
- Average landscaping job = $2,500
- You need just ONE extra job to pay for the website (0.16 jobs)
- Most landscapers get 3-5 extra jobs in Month 1 (that's $7,500-12,500)
- After that, it's pure profit forever

So you're not spending $399. You're investing $399 to make $10K+. Does that math work for you?"
```

**Feel-Felt-Found:**
```
"I understand how you feel. $399 is a real investment for a small business.

Other landscapers felt the same way initially. They thought, 'That's 2-3 hours of work.'

But what they found was that the website brought in 3-5 EXTRA jobs in the first month alone. One guy made $15,000 extra in Month 1. That's a 37x return.

Would a 37x return on your money work for you?"
```

**Anchoring Technique:**
```
"I get it. Can I share some context?

Big agencies charge $5,000-10,000 for the exact same thing I'm building for you. Fiverr charges $300-600 and takes 3-4 weeks.

I'm at $399 and you're live in 7 days. I keep it affordable because I specialize in landscapers and use tools that let me work fast.

If I was charging what agencies charge, this would be $5K. But I'm not trying to get rich on one project — I want to build a relationship where you refer me to other landscapers.

Does $399 feel more reasonable when you know the alternative is $5-10K?"
```

### 2. "I NEED TO THINK ABOUT IT" (Stall Objection)

**Root Cause:** Uncertainty, lack of urgency, OR polite rejection

**LAER Response:**
```
Listen: [full response]
Acknowledge: "Of course — this is a business decision."
Explore: "What specifically are you thinking about? Cost, timing, or whether it'll actually work?"

[Then address their SPECIFIC concern]
```

**Assumptive Close:**
```
"Totally understand. Quick question before you go — what specifically are you thinking about?

[They answer]

Got it. Let me address that right now so you can make a decision:
[Handle their specific concern]

So if I can solve [concern], are you ready to move forward today?"
```

**Urgency Injection:**
```
"I hear you. Just so you know — spring season starts in 3 weeks. Most landscapers book 60-70% of their annual revenue in March-May.

Every day you wait is a day potential customers are calling your competitors instead of you.

If you start today, you're live by [date] — right before the rush. If you wait 2 weeks to decide, you miss the first half of spring.

What questions can I answer right now so you don't miss that window?"
```

### 3. "I'M TOO BUSY RIGHT NOW" (Time Objection)

**Root Cause:** Fear of time commitment, OR they're actually busy

**Feel-Felt-Found:**
```
"I totally understand how you feel. You're running a business and every minute counts.

Other contractors felt the same way — they thought this would be a huge time investment.

But what they found was shocking: They spent 30 minutes total. That's it.

Here's the breakdown:
- 10 minutes: Send me 10 photos
- 15 minutes: Review the site I build
- 5 minutes: Approve and go live

30 minutes. That's less time than one job estimate.

And then it works for you 24/7, bringing in leads while you're doing actual work.

Worth 30 minutes to add $5K/month in revenue?"
```

**Reframe:**
```
"I hear you — you're busy because business is good. That's actually the PERFECT time to do this.

Here's why: When you're busy, you can't answer every call. Right now, how many calls do you miss per week? [They answer]

Those are lost customers. A website with a contact form captures those leads even when you're on a job site. They fill out the form, you call them back when you're free.

You're losing money by being TOO busy. Website fixes that.

Make sense?"
```

### 4. "I GET ALL MY WORK FROM REFERRALS" (Don't Need It Objection)

**Root Cause:** Not seeing the incremental value

**The Math:**
```
"That's awesome — referrals are the best customers. Keep doing that.

Quick question: How many referral customers do you get per month? [They answer — say 10]

Got it. So 10 referral customers = good, steady business.

Now imagine you ALSO get 5 customers from Google searches who've never heard of you. Now you have 15 customers.

That's 50% more revenue for the exact same amount of work. Because the website brings them to YOU.

Referrals don't go away. You just ADD to them.

Does that make sense — you're not replacing what works, you're adding a new channel?"
```

**The Trust Factor:**
```
"Here's something most contractors don't know: 73% of people check a company's website EVEN IF they were referred.

So when someone refers you, what's the first thing the person does? They Google you.

If you don't have a website, 7 out of 10 referrals don't call. They think, 'If they don't even have a website, are they legit?'

So you're actually LOSING referrals by not having a site.

Website doesn't replace referrals. It makes referrals actually convert.

Want to see the stats I'm quoting? I can send them."
```

### 5. "MY NEPHEW/FRIEND SAID THEY'D BUILD IT" (Someone Else Objection)

**Root Cause:** Loyalty, OR they want it for free

**The Respectful Pivot:**
```
"That's awesome! If they can deliver something professional and fast, definitely go with them. Family's important.

Just be aware — and I'm not trying to bash your nephew — but most business owners I talk to have been waiting 6+ months for that to happen. Meanwhile, competitors are getting those customers.

Here's my offer: Give them 2 weeks. If they deliver, great. If not, I'll have you live in 7 days for $399.

Fair?"
```

**The Opportunity Cost:**
```
"I hear you. Quick question: How long have they been saying they'll build it? [They answer]

And how many customers have you lost in that time to competitors who DO have websites?

Average landscaping job = $2,500. If you've lost even 4 jobs in [time period], that's $10,000 in lost revenue.

Your nephew might build it for free. But 'free' is costing you $10K.

My question: Would you rather save $399 or make $10,000?

Because that's the actual trade-off here."
```

### 6. "DO YOU HAVE REFERENCES?" (Trust Objection)

**Root Cause:** They don't know you, need social proof

**The Honest Answer:**
```
"Great question. I'm just launching in Woodinville, so I'm building my local client base right now.

Here's what I can offer to earn your trust:

1. I'll build your site first. You pay AFTER you approve it. Zero risk.
2. I'll show you examples of sites I've built (same quality you'll get).
3. If you're not happy, don't pay. That simple.

Sound fair?

Also — since you're one of my first Woodinville clients, I'm locking you in at $399 forever. Once I have 10 locals, I'm raising it to $599. Early-adopter pricing.

Want to see some examples?"
```

**Social Proof Substitute:**
```
"I don't have local testimonials yet, but I can show you:
1. Sites I've built for landscapers in other cities (exact same quality)
2. Before/after traffic stats (one client went from 0 to 50 calls/month)
3. My process step-by-step so you know exactly what you're getting

Which would help you decide?"
```

### 7. "SEND ME MORE INFORMATION" (Brush-Off Objection)

**Root Cause:** Either genuine interest OR polite rejection

**The Direct Response:**
```
"Absolutely. What specifically would help you decide?

I can send:
- Examples of sites for other landscapers
- Breakdown of what's included (features list)
- Timeline (what happens when)
- Customer results (traffic stats, leads generated)

Which matters most to you?"
```

**The Video Offer:**
```

Way easier than reading a bunch of PDFs.

Want me to send that? Then we can hop on a quick call if you have questions."
```

**The Assumptive Close:**
```
"Happy to send info. Quick question first: If what I send answers your questions, are you ready to move forward this week?

[If yes]
"Perfect. I'll send the package now. Let's schedule a 10-minute call for tomorrow at [time] to go over any questions. Does that work?"

[If no]
"Got it. What else do you need to feel comfortable saying yes?"
```

### 8. "I'LL BUILD IT MYSELF ON WIX/SQUARESPACE" (DIY Objection)

**Root Cause:** Want to save money, think it's easy

**The Time Math:**
```
"That's definitely an option. Have you ever built a site on Wix before? [They answer]

Here's what to expect if you go that route:
- 15-20 hours of your time learning the platform
- Another 10-15 hours building the actual site
- 5-10 hours debugging why it doesn't look right on mobile
- Total: 30-45 hours

Your hourly rate as a landscaper is probably $75-150/hour, right? So you're spending $2,250-6,750 in YOUR time to save $399.

Plus, DIY sites don't rank on Google without SEO. So you build it, but no one finds it.

My question: Would you rather spend 45 hours learning Wix, or 30 minutes with me and be live in a week with a site that actually ranks?

What makes more sense for your business?"
```

**The Quality Gap:**
```
"You can definitely try Wix. Just know this:

DIY sites look like DIY sites. Customers can tell.

When someone's choosing between you and a competitor, and your site looks homemade while theirs looks professional, who do you think they call?

First impressions matter. You get one shot.

For $399, you get a site that looks like you paid $5K. That's the difference."
```

## RESEARCH-BACKED TECHNIQUES

### Technique 1: Negative Reverse Selling

**What:** Suggest they DON'T buy if criteria not met

**Example:**
```
"Actually, let me ask you this: Are you actively looking to GROW your business right now?

[If yes] "Perfect, then this makes sense."

[If no] "Then honestly, you probably don't need a website. If you're happy with current revenue and don't want to grow, save your money. This is for people who want more customers."

[Usually they backpedal and say they DO want growth]
```

**Why it works:** Removes sales pressure, makes THEM chase YOU

### Technique 2: The Takeaway

**What:** Remove the offer if they're not serious

**Example:**
```
Prospect: "I need to think about it for a month."

Response: "I hear you. Just so you know, I'm only taking 3 more clients in February, and I have 2 slots left. If you wait a month, I'll likely be booked through April.

Are you okay potentially waiting until May to start?"
```

**Why it works:** Creates urgency through scarcity

### Technique 3: Trial Close (Test Readiness)

**What:** Ask a buying question before asking for the sale

**Example:**
```
"If we move forward, would you want to be live by [date] or [later date]?"

[If they answer] → They're mentally buying
[If they deflect] → More objections to handle
```

### Technique 4: The Ben Franklin Close

**What:** Draw a T-chart of pros vs cons together

**Example:**
```
"Let's do this: Grab a piece of paper. Draw a line down the middle.

Left side: Reasons TO get a website
Right side: Reasons NOT to get a website

Let's fill it out together. You tell me what to write.

[Walk through together]

Now look at both sides. Which side has more weight?"
```

**Why it works:** They convince themselves
