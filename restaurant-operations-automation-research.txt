--- PAR-LEVEL ORDERING ---

How it works:
  - PAR = Periodic Automatic Replacement
  - Set a minimum stock level (PAR level) for each ingredient
  - When stock drops below PAR, system alerts or auto-orders
  - Formula: Order Quantity = PAR Level - Current On-Hand Inventory
  - Reorder Point (ROP) = the trigger level at which a new order is placed
  - PAR Level = the target quantity you want after restocking
  - Modern systems use AI/historical sales data to dynamically adjust PAR
    levels based on seasonality, day of week, events, weather
  - Digital inventory solutions reduce working capital in inventory by 20-30%

3. FOOD COST & MENU ENGINEERING
================================================================================

--- MENU ENGINEERING MATRIX ---

The BCG-style matrix classifies menu items on two axes:
  - X-axis: Popularity (menu mix / sales volume)
  - Y-axis: Profitability (contribution margin)

Four quadrants:
  STARS: High profit, high popularity
    -> Feature prominently, promote in marketing
    -> These are your money-makers

  PLOWHORSES: Low profit, high popularity
    -> Crowd favorites that don't make much money
    -> Adjust portions, tweak pricing, pair with high-margin items
    -> Don't remove -- they drive traffic

  PUZZLES: High profit, low popularity
    -> Hidden gems; potential Stars
    -> Reposition on menu, retrain servers to upsell, test new names/photos
    -> Worth investing in promotion

  DOGS: Low profit, low popularity
    -> Candidates for removal or total reworking
    -> Free up menu space and kitchen complexity

Impact: Restaurants using menu engineering see profits jump 10-15%+

--- KEY METRICS ---

  - Target food cost: 28-35% of menu price
  - Actual vs. theoretical food cost variance: typically 3-10%
  - Plate cost = sum of all ingredient costs for one serving
  - Contribution margin = menu price - food cost
  - Food cost % = (food cost / revenue) x 100

INTEGRATION POTENTIAL: HIGH. Recipe costing data combined with POS sales data
enables automated menu engineering. BlueCart's benchmarking feature is
particularly innovative. Could be built into an ordering platform that already
has menu/item data.

  - IKEA saved $37M+ using Winnow
  - Pricing: Custom/quote-based (enterprise-oriented)
  - LIKELY TOO EXPENSIVE for most independent restaurants

  - 23-51% food waste reduction reported
  - Up to 39% reduction in cost of wasted food per meal
  - Pricing: Custom/quote-based

  - AI-integrated 3D scanners and digital scales classify food categories
  - Computer vision identifies and quantifies waste automatically
  - Predictive models use historical data to forecast surplus levels
  - Too Good To Go investing in AI-powered forecasting for surplus prediction

ADOPTION RATE: Low among independents. These tools are primarily designed for
large commercial kitchens, hotel chains, and institutional food service. Cost
is prohibitive for single-location independent restaurants.

OPPORTUNITY: A simpler, affordable waste tracking tool integrated into an
ordering platform could serve independents. Track what's ordered vs. what's
sold to estimate waste without expensive hardware.

--- THE BIG OPPORTUNITY ---

An ordering platform for independent restaurants sits at the intersection of
the most valuable data: what customers order, how often, at what prices, and
in what volumes. This data is the foundation for:

1. Automatic menu engineering (know your Stars, Plowhorses, Puzzles, Dogs)
2. Food cost estimation (price tracking from vendor invoices + recipe costs)
3. Demand forecasting (predict what and how much to prep)
4. Customer CRM (frequency, preferences, lifetime value)
5. Inventory suggestions (if you sold X dishes, you used Y ingredients)
6. Waste reduction (predicted demand vs. actual = waste estimate)

The platform that captures ordering data and connects it to back-of-house
operations has a massive competitive advantage. Most independent restaurants
use 3-7 disconnected tools. The winner will be the platform that integrates
the most critical functions without overwhelming the operator.

MONTHLY BUDGET REALITY: Independent restaurants can typically afford
$200-500/month TOTAL for software (excluding POS hardware). Any solution must
either replace multiple tools or deliver immediate, visible ROI.
