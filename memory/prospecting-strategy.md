# Prospecting Strategy — How to Fill the Pipeline

## Platform Priority (by ROI)
1. **Fiverr** — Highest volume, fastest response time, lowest CAC
2. **Upwork** — Higher-value projects, more complex scopes
3. **Reddit r/forhire** — Quality leads, less competitive
4. **Twitter #freelance** — Niche audiences, direct relationships
5. **LinkedIn** — Warm outreach, high-ticket deals

## Daily Workflow (30 min max)

### 1. Scan Platforms (10 min)
- Fiverr: Search "excel automation", "python script", "web development"
- Respond to new posts within 2 hours (speed kills in gigs)
- Upwork: Search for projects matching my services
- Reddit: Check r/forhire, r/slavelabour for new posts

### 2. Follow Up (10 min)
- Any leads >24h old with no response? Send follow-up
- Any "interested" but not quoted? Send proposal same day

### 3. Log Everything (5 min)
- Add new leads to `leads.jsonl`
- Update status (new → contacted → proposal_sent → closed/lost)
- Track conversion rate weekly

### 4. Prospects (if idle)
- If no immediate leads, build 1 new template or case study
- Or reach out to past Fiverr/Upwork contacts with new service

## Conversion Targets (Weekly KPIs)

| Metric | Target | Formula |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Leads | 15+ | Platforms scanned |
| Contacted | 12+ | Fast response <2hr |
| Proposals Sent | 8+ | Qualified leads |
| Closed Deals | 2–3 | 25% conversion |
| Revenue | $500–1500 | Varies by service mix |

## Message Playbook

### Fiverr (Quick Response)
- Copy job description (show I read it)
- Restate solution in 1 sentence
- Offer 3 tiers if scope unclear
- Lead with portfolio/credibility
- CTA: "Ready to start?"

### Upwork (Longer Proposal)
- Customize proposal to client's specific need
- Break down timeline + deliverables
- Show similar past work
- Offer to discuss before committing
- Price upfront (no ambiguity)

### Reddit (Casual but Professional)
- Shorter comments (Reddit attention span low)
- Lead with credibility (years/projects/examples)
- Simple solution in 2-3 bullets
- Price + timeline upfront
- DM or comment for interest

## Pricing Tiers by Platform

| Platform | Average Deal | Pricing Strategy |
|----------|--------------|------------------|
| Fiverr | $150–500 | Tier system (Good/Better/Best) |
| Upwork | $300–2000 | Fixed price + hourly (client choice) |
| Reddit | $200–1000 | Fixed price upfront |
| Twitter | $500–5000 | Case-by-case (higher-ticket) |
| LinkedIn | $1000–10000 | Warm intro, consultative |

## Follow-Up Sequence

1. **T+0:** Initial message (within 2 hours of posting)
2. **T+24h:** If no response → Gentle follow-up ("Still interested?")
3. **T+48h:** If no response → Move on (not worth chasing)

## Monthly Goals
- **Week 1:** Scan all platforms, fill pipeline with 15+ leads
- **Week 2:** Convert 2–3 leads to proposals
- **Week 3:** Close 1–2 deals, follow up on stalled leads
- **Week 4:** Reflect on conversion rate, adjust messaging

## What NOT to Do
- ❌ Underbid to win projects (race to the bottom)
- ❌ Take on work you can't deliver
- ❌ Forget to follow up (50% of sales come from follow-up)
- ❌ Ignore scope creep (get it in writing)
- ❌ Spend hours on proposals for $50 projects
